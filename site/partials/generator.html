Study info

This study is being conducted by Jamie Birch for the evaluation of his MSc Computer Science dissertation project on optimising the study of technical/topic-specific vocabulary in Japanese. He is a student in the Engineering Department of the University of Bristol, under the supervision of Prof. Peter Flach.

The study consists of three parts:

  1. Taking a quiz to measure current level of comprehension of a topic [est. 7 minutes]
  2. Studying from a vocabulary list [as long or as short as you choose; eg. cramming in 30 minutes, or revising over several days]
  3. Taking another quiz to measure any changes in comprehension of the topic [est. 7 minutes]

I shall be asking participants to fill in a small demographic survey (below) so that demographic effects may be measured. Note that nonetheless, most fields are optional.

Data stored is limited to: the user-provided demographic information; the website-provided access ID; the website-generated vocabulary list; timestamps of events; user feedback; and the user's performance during the website-generated quizzes as well as the quizzes themselves. Information on unstated data such as IP address will not be taken.

Data protection

All data begins and will remain anonymous (eg. phrasing in the dissertation will resemble the format of: "participant #3, who claimed a high level of environmental exposure to Japanese") and confidential. Data will be stored on my password-protected home computer, which is accessible only by myself.

Participants may request a PDF copy of the final dissertation, which is scheduled to be submitted on 15th September and - to my understanding - will be stored in print in the University of Bristol library.

Participants have a right to have their data withdrawn from the study upon request until 20th August, whereupon the data shall be considered usable in the final dissertation write-up.
